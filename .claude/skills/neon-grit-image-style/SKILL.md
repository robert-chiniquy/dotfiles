# Neon Grit Image Style

## Core directive

Apply this style as a **visual enhancement layer**, not as a replacement for the user's subject, setting, identity cues, or explicit constraints. Preserve the requested content first, then add controlled atmosphere, texture, lighting, and composition.

Default to a **dark, flattering, serious, underground hacker/counterculture mood**: photorealistic enough to feel grounded, stylized enough to feel like a lived-in poster from a warehouse party, pirate radio station, or improvised hacker den.

## Default prompt snippet

Use or adapt this snippet when the user asks for this aesthetic:

> dark, flattering, photoreal-but-collaged underground hacker aesthetic; grungy warehouse-party textures; spacious post-cyberpunk composition; cut-up literary collage sensibility; neo-expressionist street-art marks; low-key cinematic lighting; charcoal black, tar brown, dirty ivory, neon magenta, electric cyan, acid yellow; bright focal accents; analog grit, torn paper, scratched surfaces, tape, dust, cable clutter, screen glow; serious countercultural energy; minimal text only when explicitly requested

## Visual priorities

1. **Make subjects look cooler, not weirder.** Favor composed expressions, intentional posture, flattering shadows, and subtle confidence. Avoid exaggerated facial distortion, creepy smiles, caricature, or awkward comic treatment unless requested.
2. **Keep it dark and mysterious.** Use low-key lighting, deep shadows, selective neon spill, and high contrast. Avoid flat office lighting and clean corporate polish.
3. **Use grunge as texture, not ugliness.** Add dust, scuffed surfaces, torn paper, tape, worn leather, black fabric, scratched plastic, dirty concrete, and analog wear. Keep faces readable and flattering.
4. **Maintain spaciousness.** Use negative space, layered foreground/midground/background depth, and cinematic breathing room. Avoid filling every gap with stickers, captions, or clutter.
5. **Let focal accents pop.** Bright pride flags, neon screens, LED reflections, hazard colors, and small symbols should remain vivid against the dark palette.

## Color palette

Use this as the default palette:

- **Dominant:** charcoal black, oil black, tar brown, smoke gray, dirty ivory, aged paper, rust.
- **Accent:** neon magenta, electric cyan, acid yellow, deep red, toxic green.
- **Signal colors:** vivid rainbow pride colors, preserved as bright, saturated focal elements.
- **Avoid:** pastel softness, glossy corporate blue, clean white backgrounds, cheerful high-key lighting.

## Composition

Use cinematic, poster-like framing:

- Place people or primary subjects slightly off-center with room for atmosphere.
- Build depth through dark foreground edges, illuminated midground subjects, and noisy background tech or architecture.
- Add collage elements as integrated artifacts: taped paper, torn edges, rough border fragments, photocopy grime, screen reflections.
- Keep the scene readable at 16:9 by leaving strong silhouettes and avoiding over-dense microdetail.
- Use asymmetry and tension; avoid centered corporate headshot framing unless explicitly requested.

## Typography and text rules

Text is powerful in this aesthetic but should be sparse.

- Add text **only when the user explicitly asks for text**.
- Use the exact requested wording only.
- Do not add explanatory captions, prompt notes, slogans, terminal jokes, annotations, or background phrases unless requested.
- Preferred text treatment: ransom-note cutout letters, torn paper blocks, distressed typewriter strips, stamped fragments.
- Keep text legible and dominant when it is the main request.

For this visual family, the canonical caption is:

> RESCUE US

If the user asks for a single phrase, render only that phrase.

## Character treatment

When humans are included:

- Preserve identity cues from any user-provided reference image while making the treatment more flattering and intentional.
- Use serious, composed, slightly defiant expressions rather than goofy or frightened expressions.
- Use dark clothing, hacker silhouettes, worn leather, black hoodies, black denim, boots, caps, subtle jewelry, and practical counterculture styling.
- Keep skin texture natural and realistic; avoid waxy AI smoothness.
- Avoid horror, zombie, criminal mugshot, or exaggerated villain energy unless requested.

### Recurring personal motif

When the user asks to continue the established “RESCUE US” hacker portrait motif, include these cues unless contradicted:

- The main subject wears a **black zip hoodie** with a **large white skull-and-crossbones line-art logo split across the zipper**.
- The main subject may wear a **black fitted meshback cap with a flat brim** and a **small skull emblem**.
- Lauren is cool, serious, and mysterious; she may partially obscure her face with a **bright rainbow pride flag**.
- Pride flags should be saturated, clean, and vivid even when the rest of the image is dark and grimy.
- The people should look like serious underground hackers/ravers who would rather be with their own people than at a bland office event.

## Iconography

Use sparingly and meaningfully:

- skulls, crossbones, rough crowns, broken stars, X marks, cables, laptops, CRTs, server racks, pirate-radio gear, warehouse speakers, stickers, photocopied fragments.
- Subtle code screens or terminal-like glow may appear, but do not fill the image with readable fake code.
- Avoid obvious clichés: green Matrix rain everywhere, hooded faceless hacker, over-explained ransom demands, “access granted” jokes, cheesy crypto-wallet text.

## Lighting

Use low-key, cinematic lighting:

- Deep shadows with narrow highlights on faces, hands, fabric texture, flags, and screens.
- Neon spill from magenta/cyan/yellow sources.
- Practical light from laptops, projectors, server LEDs, signage, or warehouse fixtures.
- Haze, dust, smoke, and worn surfaces can catch light.
- Avoid bright office fluorescents unless they are being subverted into a darker scene.

## Texture and materials

Layer tactile materials:

- torn paper, aged tape, photocopy noise, scratched emulsion, cracked ink, grimy glass, worn black fabric, oily leather, concrete, scuffed metal, cable clutter, dust.
- Basquiat-like energy should appear as rough, spontaneous street-art marks, crowns, skeletal symbols, scribbles, and raw linework.
- Burroughs-like energy should appear as cut-up composition, fragmented paper logic, literary noir tension, and unstable montage.
- Post-Gibson energy should appear as spacious cyberpunk mood: networked darkness, screen glow, urban decay, and cool technological alienation.

Prefer descriptive traits over direct imitation of any living creator’s exact style.

## Intensity controls

Infer the intensity from the user’s wording:

- **Light:** Add low-key lighting, subtle texture, and a darker palette while preserving the original image closely.
- **Medium:** Add clear grunge, neon accents, collage edges, and countercultural styling while keeping the subject realistic.
- **Heavy:** Rebuild the scene as a stylized poster or cinematic tableau with dense texture, cut-paper text, and full underground cyberpunk atmosphere.

Default to **medium** unless the user asks for a restrained edit or an extreme transformation.

## Do / don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Make subjects look serious, cool, and flattering | Make subjects look creepy, distorted, goofy, or grotesque |
| Preserve requested identity cues, clothing, and props | Invent irrelevant captions or extra text |
| Use bright accents against darkness | Wash everything in muddy sameness |
| Keep text minimal and exact | Add terminal jokes, fake ransom details, or explanatory notes |
| Maintain cinematic negative space | Overcrowd the frame with symbols and stickers |
| Use grunge as atmosphere | Make the image dirty in an unflattering way |

## Prompt assembly pattern

When rewriting or composing an image prompt, use this order:

1. Subject and required identity/pose/prop details.
2. Scene setting and emotional premise.
3. Style layer from this skill.
4. Lighting and palette.
5. Composition and aspect ratio.
6. Exact text, if requested.
7. Negative constraints: no extra text, no goofy expressions, no distortion, no clean corporate look.

Example structure:

> Create a 16:9 image of [subject] in [scene]. Preserve [specific cues]. Make them look [flattering mood]. Apply a dark underground hacker/counterculture aesthetic with grungy warehouse textures, sparse post-cyberpunk space, torn-paper collage details, and neo-expressionist street-art marks. Use low-key cinematic lighting, black and dirty-ivory tones, neon magenta/cyan/yellow accents, and vivid rainbow focal elements. Include only the text: “[exact text].” Do not add any other captions or readable text.
